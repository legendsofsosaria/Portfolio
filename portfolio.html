Custom Features Developed for Ultima Online

The remaining videos showcase some of the custom systems and features I have conceptualized, designed, and programmed for Ultima Online, a classic multiplayer online role-playing game (MMORPG). The features displayed demonstrate my skills in game development, including systems programming, UI design, and creating engaging mechanics that enhance player interaction and immersion. Tools Used: C#, CentrED# (for mapping), UOFiddler (software for editing client files), VitaNex API's and ServUO emulator. Here are some highlights of the custom systems I developed:

Diving System:

I created a custom diving system that allows players to travel underwater. This system involved designing a terrain for underwater travel, creating animations for water bubbles, and implementing a drowning mechanic that is prevented by equipping a scuba mask.

Seed Machine:

I designed and implemented a seed machine that processes crops into seeds. The machine has a 2-minute processing time per crop, and I implemented a timer to manage this. The custom UI updates every 2 minutes with a refreshed gump (player interface), allowing players to track the remaining time for each crop.

Harvesting System:

This system allows players to plant and harvest crops in public fields or on their house plots. A unique challenge was preventing crops from being left behind when playersâ€™ houses decayed. Additionally, crops in public fields can be picked by other players after 8 days of inaction.

Necromancy Domination System:

I created a Necromancy Domination system, allowing players to control undead creatures. This system required client-side edits for custom tooltips and enabling necromancy as an "active" skill. Additionally, the creature's behavior is affected by the player's karma, and a positive karma player cannot control the pet until their karma goes negative.

Monsters Projectiles:

I created hundreds of custom monsters, including Earth Titans that throw projectiles. Giants (Fire, Frost, Storm, Stone) each use a unique projectile with corresponding damage types, such as fireballs, ice chunks, lightning, and boulders.

Auto Blood Vial:

This showcases a quest item I programmed, designed by another staff member. The Auto Blood Vial draws blood from a monster but causes it to explode afterward. I utilized built-in VitaNex effects for this functionality.

Sunless Citadel Dungeon:

In this project, I programmed all the monsters, quests, items, and systems for a custom dungeon built by one of our developers. The dungeon includes intricate systems and unique mechanics, developed based on detailed notes from the designer.

Archaeology Museum:

I designed and mapped this dungeon, also programming all the monsters, quests, items, and systems. One key feature is the archaeology system, where players use tools to dig and uncover items. The dungeon features puzzles and traps, including a complex lever puzzle and a boulder trap that drops from the ceiling.

Achievement System:

I designed and implemented this achievement system, which rewards players for completing tasks and keeps track of their progress. Players can view their achievements, as well as others' achievements, using in-game commands. A complex part of the system was the daily login reward, which resets after 24 hours of inactivity.
